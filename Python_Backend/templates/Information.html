What is this web-app for?

In Hill Climb Racing 2 there is a system called adventure stars which are stars you can get in each map by driving as far as you can with any vehicle. The max amount of stars per vehicle per map is 10,000 and because there are 31 vehicles the maxmimum amount of stars possible in a map is 310,000. The reason this is better than the in-game version is because you can only see one map's stars at a time, whereas with this spreadsheet you can see every single map and vehicle stars at the same time which also makes it easier to see which ones have 10000 and which ones dont.



How to use the form

On the statistics page scroll down past the spreadsheet to the form underneath and input the map you played in the first box, the vehicle you played in that map in the second box, and the distance of how far you got in that run e.g 4092m. Then after those have been filled click "Submit Distance" and that info will be inserted into the database and displayed on the spreadsheet above.



Where are the tutorials and rankings?

On the left side of the information page scroll down until you find the desired map and expand it by clicking on it. This will extend a section that contains a list of every vehicles difficulty for getting 10k in that specific map, below that clicking on the vehicle name will take you to a youtube tutorial, the specific parts are right next to the vehicle name. (NOTE: 4 Parts means you must have the 4th mastery on that vehicle to equip and use the 4th part)



What are the sections at the bottom of the Statistics page?

These display the total amount of stars/distance on a specific map using the data from the spreadsheet above. It shows your current stars on a specific map out of 310,000 and also as a percentage above it



What is the "Overall Progress" section on the Satistics page?

This displays miscellaneous information relating to the spreadsheet like; How many vehicle and map combination 10ks have been completed out of the 558 total. Only vehicle and map combinations that have exactly 10,000 are counted. The first box is the total amount of distance from every vehicle and map combination. The second is how many maps have a total of 310,000 stars, this only counts when every vehicle in one map has 10,000. The third box is the amount of vehicle and map combinations remaining that dont have 10,000. The fourth box shows the average amount of stars for each map.
